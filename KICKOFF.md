# How to Talk to Claude — Your Magic Phrases

You don't need to remember any setup. Just say one of these.

---

## 1. To start something new, say:

> **"New book idea"**

Then tell Claude the idea (her words are great as-is). Claude will:
1. Create a fresh folder in `projects/` from the TEMPLATE.
2. Drop her idea into `brief.md` exactly as she said it.
3. Start `story.md` and wait for more before shaping anything.
4. Add the project to `projects/INDEX.md` so it's easy to find later.
5. Keep it completely separate from every other idea.

*Optional:* say "New book idea — call it [name]". If you don't name it, Claude will suggest one.

---

## 2. To go back to an older one, say:

> **"Let's edit [name]"**   or   **"Let's change the premise of [name]"**

Claude will open that project, show you where things stand, and make the changes. You can edit the premise, the characters, the story — anything. If you can't remember the name, just say **"show me our projects"** and Claude will list them from `INDEX.md`.

---

## 3. To work on her voice, say:

> **"Add a voice"** — tell me an author she loves and what she likes about them. I add a card to `shared/voices.md`.

> **"Voice session"** — we sit with her writing and notice what's becoming *hers*. The real prize.

When starting or editing a book, you can also say **"lean this one on [author]"** and I'll note it as an influence in that book's brief — feel and technique only, never their actual words.

---

## 4. To save or go back to versions, say:

> **"Save a draft"** — I freeze the current state into a dated snapshot in that book's `drafts/` folder. Nothing is ever lost.

> **"Restore [snapshot name]"** — I bring an older frozen version back to the live files.

We're experimenting, so we save often. The live files are always current; `drafts/` holds the restore points.

---

## Why it's built this way
She'll bring you lots of ideas — so this is made to be *fast*. One phrase in, one folder out, nothing ever bleeds between projects. Every idea is safe, separate, and easy to find again. Voices guide her, but the goal is always *her* voice.
