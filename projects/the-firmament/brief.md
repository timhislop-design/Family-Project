# Book Brief — THE FIRMAMENT (working title)

*Founding version, Tim & Claude, Jul 23, 2026. Abby's section deliberately left open — her words get added verbatim when she's ready, and they outrank ours.*

## The big idea (in her words)
> *(Waiting for Abby. This space is hers.)*

## The big idea (how it stands today)
The world is a wide golden land under the vault of heaven — a dome sky with waters above it, stars that are lights, wandering stars everyone knows are angels because you can watch them move, and a sun that crosses on schedule. The faith is real. Prayers are answered. Sing the harvest blessing and the windows of heaven open and it rains. This is not superstition and the book never treats it as superstition — it works, every time, and it has worked for two thousand years.

It works because the scripture is a mission document worn smooth by a hundred generations of retelling, and the liturgy is a command language that still parses. Nobody knows this. "Amen" means *execute*. The oldest prayers are instruction strings. Archaic cosmology — dome, waters above, moving lights — is engineering described accurately by people who no longer knew they were describing engineering.

## What kind of book is it?
Teen thriller/adventure with a slow-burn mystery engine, for Abby's age up through adults with imagination. Book 1 runs **golden**: festivals, community, pride, the ache and warmth of a world that deserves to be loved — with exactly one thread of wrongness that tightens all year. The cold arrives only in the last act. Built to translate to the screen (the dome dawn, the Raising ceremony, the ritual that goes wrong, the final image).

## Who's in it?
- **Selah** (placeholder-final; the lost word of the Psalms — a girl named for liturgy nobody can translate). Fifteen. A **canter**: the keepers who hold the unabridged old forms in memory. She's the best of her generation and everyone knows it, which is part of her problem — the town treats her as blessed (prodigy, *and* sister of a Taken) and she feels like a fraud wearing a borrowed face. Awkward everywhere except inside the liturgy, the one place she's sure. Anxious-first-then-rises. **Her secret:** the night before her brother's Raising, a small girl showing off for God sang the oldest blessing she knew over his sleeping face. He was chosen at dawn. Everyone called it grace. She has spent seven years quietly wondering if she called it down — and her ferocious devotion is atonement she has never said out loud.
- **Her brother** (placeholder name **Amos** — "carried"). Taken seven years ago at the Raising, the highest honor a family can receive. Warm, unremarkable, hers. Everything the doctrine says about where he went is beautiful.
- **The Returned man** (placeholder name open). In living memory, exactly one Taken came back — and came back wrong. Holy and untouchable and ruined; the town keeps him like a relic and nobody meets his eyes. He was Taken **the same year as her brother.** He barely speaks — except sometimes he cants, letter-perfect, in old forms even Selah doesn't have. Talking to him is her first real transgression.
- **Her mother.** Built her whole standing on being mother of a Taken. Loves Selah fiercely and terrifyingly through the lens of what the family owes heaven now.
- **The high priest** (title TBD). Not a cartoon. In the opening ceremony, when the Voice does the impossible, Selah sees his face — and understands that someone already knew it could do that. What he knows, and what it costs him, is Act III material.
- **Friends / the ordinary kids** — to be built with Abby (her casting pass, like The Deep's town kids).

## Where does it happen?
The land under the firmament — working texture: harvest country, one great temple town, villages, a rim of mountains no one crosses. Everything warm, worn, loved. Scale of the land = deliberately vague to readers early. All naming open for Abby.

## What happens? (Book 1 in a few sentences)
At the year's highest ritual, Selah is given the honor of the recitation and — because she's her — sings the *oldest* form, letter-perfect. The Voice, which has said the same twelve sentences for two thousand years, answers with something extra: it asks her something back. The Voice does not ask. Pulling that one thread through a golden year — the old forms answering differently than the worn ones, the Returned man who cants in his sleep, the blessing that wasn't a blessing — she arrives at the midpoint knife (**what her childhood blessing actually was**) and, in the cold last act, at the first seam in the sky. The book ends with her knowing the world has a door, that her brother is on the other side of it, and that she is the only person alive who speaks the language the door listens to.

## The engine
Her gift caused her wound. The blessing that took Amos and the memory that finds the seams are the same faculty — so the search for the truth and the search for her brother are the same search, and she can never put one down without dropping the other.

## The core question (the knife, esp. for adult readers)
**If the prayers are answered, does it matter who answers?** The Voice is real, it hears her, it has kept every promise for two thousand years. The book never sneers at her faith and never rescues it. Her tragedy is that she was *right* — something up there listens — and still everything she was told about it is wrong. Theme = the house theme, refusing the inherited story — but her version is new: everyone else refuses by doubting; **Selah refuses by believing harder than the institution does.** Her heresy is fidelity. Follow the scripture more literally than anyone alive and you find the seams the centuries painted over.

## Things we want to make sure we include
- The Raising (ceremony name WIP): the selection is **longed for**, not feared — the anti-reaping. Mothers weep with pride. That inversion is ours; protect it.
- Answered prayer on the page early and often, played warm and real. The reader should love this world before they fear for it.
- Textual criticism as adventure: her "power" is noticing. Every discovery earned by attention, none given by blood or prophecy.
- The wandering stars / visible angels; rain as the windows of heaven opening; at least one festival built around it.
- No deaths in Book 1 (matching The Deep's ladder) unless the three of us decide otherwise.

## Influences for *this* book
Silo / Hunger Games / The 100 for bones; Bible + ancient-aliens material for blood; tone target = golden pastoral over a hum of wrongness. Feel and technique only — see `differentiation.md` for what we deliberately do NOT take from each.
