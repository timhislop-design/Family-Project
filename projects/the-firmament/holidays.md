# THE FIRMAMENT — The Festival Calendar
*(House trick from The Deep: one festival anchoring each act. All names WIP — Abby's naming pass wanted. The rule for this book: every festival is warm and beloved on the page, and every festival is secretly a schedule.)*

## The Opening of the Windows — first rain (Act I anchor, candidate)
The whole town sings the request together at the season's turn, and the windows of heaven open, and it rains — on schedule, every year, forever. Umbrellas as celebration; kids soaked on purpose; the first puddle-stomp of the year is practically a sacrament. The warmest big scene in Act I. (Underneath, never said: it's the irrigation schedule, and the song is the request string.)

## The Raising — the choosing (the book's hinge; name WIP)
The annual ceremony where the Voice speaks the names of the chosen and they are taken up to heaven. **The anti-reaping: everyone longs to be chosen.** Mothers weep with pride. Families of the Taken are honored forever. Selah's brother was Raised seven years ago; this year's Raising is where the book opens, with Selah given the honor of the recitation — and the Voice asking something back.

## The night of the wandering stars (Act II candidate; name WIP)
The festival where the town stays up to watch the angels cross the dome. Kids count them and make wishes; lovers name them; old people say which angel carried which ancestor's prayer. The sky as proof, once a year. (This is the festival where a wrong thing should happen quietly — a star that pauses — seen only by Selah.)

## The Act III festival — the one that goes publicly wrong
House pattern (mirrors The Deep): the year's last great ritual fails in front of everyone. Which festival that is — harvest thanksgiving? a remembrance for the Days of Smoke? — is open, and Abby should have a big say in a holiday she invents.
