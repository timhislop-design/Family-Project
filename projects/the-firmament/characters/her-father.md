# Her Father
*(Barely defined on purpose — this one is mostly Abby's.)*

## The little that's canon
He exists, he's alive, and he is *quieter* than his wife about the family's honor — which could be humility, or could be something else. In a book about a mother who stands in the light of the Taking, a father who doesn't quite look at it is interesting.

## The fork (Abby decides, with Dad)
- **Option A — the quiet doubter:** he loved his son more than the honor, has never said so, and Selah has half-noticed. Gives her one potential ally in the house — and one more person with an unsaid thing.
- **Option B — the true believer, gentler flavor:** his faith is soft and real and completely unquestioning, which is somehow harder for Selah than her mother's fierceness. Nobody to ally with; the loneliness deepens.
- **Option C — absent:** dead or gone before the story, and the house is the two women and the empty chair. Cleanest cast, biggest hole.

## Open questions (for Abby)
- Which fork? (Or wreck all three.)
- His name and his work?
- One memory of him being an actual dad — it should exist, and hurt a little. (House pattern: the same ask worked beautifully for Nerai's father.)
