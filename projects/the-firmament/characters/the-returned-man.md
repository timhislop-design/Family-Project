# The Returned Man
*(No name yet — deliberately. The town doesn't use one, which tells you everything. His true name and what actually happened to him are candidates for Abby's pass and the vault respectively.)*

## What the town knows
In living memory, exactly one Taken came back. He was found at the rim road one morning, years after his Raising, and he was *wrong* — not injured, not mad exactly, just... returned, which is not a thing that happens, from a place you do not return from. The doctrine strained and settled on: *holy*. He is a relic now. He lives at the edge of town in a house nobody visits, provided for and avoided. You don't speak to him. It isn't forbidden, exactly. It's worse than forbidden — it's *not done*.

## What's wrong about him (canon so far)
He is quiet for weeks at a time. He flinches at nothing anyone else can see. And sometimes — this is the thing the kids dare each other about — he **cants in his sleep**, out on his porch in the afternoon sun: letter-perfect, in old forms so old that even Selah doesn't have them. The only person in the world fluent in the language she's given her life to — and he's the one person she's not allowed to know.

## Why he matters
He was Taken **the same year as Amos.** When Selah finally speaks to him — her first true transgression, and the hinge of Act I — she isn't chasing the mystery. She's chasing her brother. What he actually knows and what happened to him: sealed in `../canon-vault.md`.

## Craft note
Candidate for the most beloved character in the series if we do him right. The register is *tender-creepy*, never horror-movie: he should scare the kids and break the adults' hearts, ideally in the same scene. He never explains himself. He answers questions nobody asked and goes silent at the ones they did.
