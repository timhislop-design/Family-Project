# Her Mother
*(Unnamed — Abby's to name.)*

## Who she is
A **mother of the Taken** — in this world, a standing all its own: honored seating at every festival, a voice the priests defer to, the town's living proof that heaven notices. She has built her entire identity on the morning the Voice said her son's name. Losing that would not just grieve her; it would *unmake* her.

## How she loves
Fiercely, and through the lens of what the family owes heaven now. She is not cold — this is not Nerai's mother — she is *warm and terrifying at once*: proud of Selah in public, exacting in private, always watching her daughter's excellence the way you'd watch a fire in a wind. Some of it is love. Some of it is fear she has never examined. Whether she ever wonders about the cost of the honor — whether pride has a crack in it — is Act III material.

## The engine with Selah
Selah cannot tell her mother her secret, because her mother's whole world is built on the blessing being grace. Every scene between them runs on that unsaid thing. Feeling lives in concrete objects (house rule): the place set for Amos at every meal; his coat, brushed and hung, seven years clean.
