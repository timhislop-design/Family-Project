# The Canters — the guild kids and their teacher
*(Selah's world: her rivals, her colleagues, maybe her friends. Mostly Abby's to cast — this is her town-kids equivalent for this book.)*

## What the guild is
The kids trained from childhood to memorize the unabridged old forms — call-and-response drilling, error means starting over from the top, perfection as devotion. A small guild, a handful per generation. They are the town's pride and they all know each other too well, the way kids do who've spent ten years being corrected in front of each other.

## Canon so far (thin on purpose)
- **The old canter** — their teacher, who trained Selah's ear. Knows the forms are strange; has spent a lifetime deciding not to wonder why. What he suspects about his best student is open.
- **The rival** — fluent but doesn't *listen*: technically perfect, deaf to the variants, and everyone but the guild thinks he/she is Selah's equal. The contrast that makes her gift visible on the page.
