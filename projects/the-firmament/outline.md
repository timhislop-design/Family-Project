# THE FIRMAMENT — The Spine

The adjustable map for this book. Change the premise up here freely; build the story **chapter by chapter** below — hone one until it's right before opening the next.

## Premise (adjust freely)
> A girl who is the best believer in a world where belief provably works discovers that the oldest versions of the prayers — the ones only she remembers — answer differently than the ones everyone uses. The world is golden, the faith is real, the sky is a ceiling, and her brother is on the other side of it.

## Core question of the book
> If the prayers are answered, does it matter who answers?

## Shape of Book 1 (three acts, golden → cold)
- **Act I — Golden.** The world at its warmest: harvest, festivals, her family's standing, her canting triumph. The opening ceremony where the Voice *asks her something back* — one wrong note in a beautiful song. Ends with her first transgression: she speaks to the Returned man.
- **Act II — The seam.** Quiet experiments: old forms vs. worn forms, what answers and how. The Returned man's fragments. The high priest watching her. **Midpoint knife: the blessing she sang over Amos was a designation. She marked him.** Her devotion and her guilt finally have the same name.
- **Act III — The cold drop.** What she does with it. The institution notices. The year's last ritual goes publicly wrong (mirror of the opening one). Final image: the first undeniable seam in the sky — and her decision. No rescue, no door opened yet, no deaths. The reader closes Book 1 knowing what Selah knows: the world has a door, and she speaks its language.
