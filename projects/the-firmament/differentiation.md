# THE FIRMAMENT — Differentiation Audit
*(Tim's day-one worry: "I am concerned we are being too derivative." This file is the standing answer. Check every new idea against it; if a beat could be swapped into one of these franchises without anyone noticing, it needs another pass.)*

## The two moves that are structurally OURS
1. **The box is beautiful.** Every enclosed-society story runs gray (Silo's rust, The Giver's numbness, Ember's dying bulbs, The 100's brutalism). Ours is golden, warm, and *worthy of love* — the dread comes only from loving a world with a seam in it. If a chapter feels dystopian, it's off-model.
2. **Her heresy is fidelity.** Every YA lead cracks the world by doubting. Selah cracks it by believing harder and more literally than the institution. No chosen one, no prophecy, no gift-from-birth: her power is memory and attention, and every discovery is earned by noticing. If she's ever handed a revelation she didn't earn, cut it.

## Comp-by-comp
- **Silo / Wool** — closest structural comp (enclosed world, institutional lie, forbidden curiosity). Not us because: Silo is gray, adult, faithless, underground; its truth is withheld by force. Ours is golden, devout, under a sky, and the truth is hidden by *erosion*, not conspiracy — the wearing-down of words did most of the work; the institution mostly guards a fear it barely understands.
- **The Hunger Games** — the ceremony. Theirs is dreaded, a punishment; ours is longed for, the highest honor — mothers weep with pride. The anti-reaping. Also: no arena, no televised cruelty, no rebellion plot in Book 1.
- **The 100 / Ascension** — generation-vessel reveals. We deliberately did NOT make the world a ship. The dome sits on ground; the reveal isn't "we're on a ship," it's *what the schedule was for*.
- **The City of Ember** — nearest YA comp (kids decode degraded founder instructions). Not us because: Ember's world is dying and joyless and its faith is empty habit; ours is thriving and its faith WORKS. Ember's instructions are a map out; our scripture is a live interface. And Ember's reveal comes early — our Book 1 never even opens the door.
- **The Giver** — the ceremony, the engineered community. Theirs suppresses feeling; ours overflows with it. No sameness, no memory-wipe. Opposite palettes on purpose.
- **The Truman Show** — beloved false world, seam in the sky. Ours differs: no audience, no director mocking him — the machinery isn't cynical, it's *faithful*. Nobody is watching for entertainment. (The dome-seam final image risks a visual echo — stage it as beautiful/sacred, not studio-wall.)
- **The Village** — elders maintaining a protective lie. Ours differs: the elders aren't the authors of the lie and mostly believe it too; time is the author.
- **Small Gods (Pratchett)** — belief and the machinery of a god, but satire for adults. We are never satirical about her faith. The book's rule: never sneer, never rescue.
- **Raised by Wolves / Battlestar** — scripture-vs-machine themes, adult register. We stay in the teen close-POV and keep the theology as lived warmth, not debate.
- **The Mortal Instruments / the 2010s angel-YA wave** — why we did NOT build on Nephilim/Watchers directly. That shelf is crowded; ours keeps the Watcher-flavored material as deep-background fog only.
- **Percy Jackson / Harry Potter** — the branch we deliberately did not take this time (discovery fantasy is The Deep's lane). No school, no gifted kids, no taxonomy of powers — nobody in this book has powers at all.

## Standing tests before any new beat ships
1. Swap test: could this beat drop into Silo/HG/Ember unchanged? Then it's not ours yet.
2. Golden test: does Book 1 material stay warm? Dread belongs in the last act and in bookends.
3. Earned test: did Selah notice her way to this, or was it given to her?
4. Sneer test: does anything on the page treat her faith as stupid? Kill it.
5. Screen test: is there one image in this chapter you could put on a poster? (Dome dawn, the wrong answer, the weeping mothers, a star that stops.)
