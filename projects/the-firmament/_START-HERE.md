## What this is
The second book/series in the Family Project — deliberately the **other branch** of the genre The Deep lives in. The Deep is a *discovery fantasy* (the world is bigger than you were told). The Firmament is an *imprisonment thriller* (the world is smaller than you were told) — Silo/Hunger Games/The 100 bones, but golden instead of gray, and built on Bible + mythology + ancient-aliens material played completely straight.

**Premise in one breath:** A beautiful world under a dome sky where the religion provably works — prayers answered, rains on schedule, angels visible among the stars — because the scripture is a mission document worn smooth by two thousand years of retelling, and the liturgy is a command language that still parses. A devout girl with perfect memory for the oldest forms notices that the old words and the worn words *answer differently* — and follows the seam. Started 2026-07-23 (Tim & Claude jam session; premise redirect to be shared with Abby when she's ready — no clock on that).

**NO BLEED RULE:** This project shares craft rules with The Deep but zero canon. One possible far-future rhyme is noted in `canon-vault.md` and `ideas-parking-lot.md` — noted, and touched never, unless all three of us decide otherwise.

## Voice architecture (the house signature, applied here)
Same machinery The Deep proved, different narrator:
1. **Seasoned frame narrator over insecure close POV.** A voiced frame opens the book (official history of the world, told lovingly, then refused). After that: everything through **Selah's** eyes, raw and fifteen.
2. **The narrator's identity is a sealed series secret** (in `canon-vault.md`). He was naive; he learned; he's telling it years later. Never named on the page; tells planted for rereaders. Reveal timing = late series, mirroring The Deep's Iggy decision. *(Proposed by Claude Jul 23, one alternative noted in the vault — Tim to confirm.)*
3. **Bookend mechanics as in The Deep** (_START-HERE #8 there): italic narrator bookends at chapter top/bottom; body is pure Selah, character-driven, dialogue and action carrying the story; the only sanctioned narrator bleed inside the body is TIME, disguisable as her own hindsight. Journal test applies.
4. **Selah's pattern = the Nerai pattern:** anxious-first-then-rises. Constant dread and self-doubt in the commentary; when the moment comes she steps forward anyway and it surprises her. Her one island of confidence is the canting — the single place she feels sure — which is exactly where the world starts coming apart.
