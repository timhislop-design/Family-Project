# Eli Halloran — 16

**The keeper's kid who becomes a keeper the moment keeping ends.** Sixth generation on the Halloran Light, through Mum's line. Assigned the humiliating handover grunt-work: digitizing the family archive under Class C stickers.

**Voice:** journal test applies in full. Petty specific observations, teen logic, self-interruption. His humor is dry and aimed mostly at objects (the scanner is his nemesis before anything cosmic is). House pattern: anxious first, then rises — dread runs constant commentary; when the moment comes he steps forward and it surprises him.

**The wound:** he's the kid who was promised a light that won't exist. Every keeper skill he's proud of — reading drift, holding the Quiet Hour, his coming First Light — has an expiration date stamped on it, and everyone keeps being *nice* about it, which is worse. His embarrassment about the family folklore is really embarrassment about loving something the world has priced at zero.

**The turn:** the chart. The moment superstition becomes data under his own hands, the shame flips into the thing he'd fight for. His arc in one line: from *"nobody reads the log"* to *"I am the log."*

**With Maren:** close, comms-banter shorthand, the only person he almost can't lie to. Her patch (the contractor's) is the one thing they don't joke about.
**With Rhys:** the fish-hook secret sits between them for half the book — two people protecting each other from a conversation.
**With Dana:** he's watch-crew before he's son; her discipline reads as coldness until it doesn't (echo of Nerai's mother, different key).

**Stake in the ground:** he never becomes a chosen one. No gift, no destiny — his edge is that he's the only person alive who's read all hundred and forty years. Attention as inheritance. (Rhymes with Selah on purpose; the rhyme is house signature now.)
