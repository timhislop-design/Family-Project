# Rhys Halloran — Dad, married in, took the name

**The skeptic with Gran's pen in his coat.** Came out on a two-year maintenance contract twenty-some years ago; stayed for Dana; took the Halloran name at the watch book like the tradition asks. Publicly the family's tension-release valve: jokes, half-mockery of Edda's folklore ("your Gran logged weather in a vacuum, kiddo"), the reasonable man who says the company isn't wrong about the economics.

**The secret:** he's been continuing Edda's fish-hook marks for years — same notation, her refilled pen, entries timed so Dana's watch never overlaps his. Not because he believes. Because the night Edda died mid-volume, somebody had to finish the page, and then somebody had to keep finishing it. His marks are grief practiced as record-keeping — which means Rhys is the theme (witness as love) hiding inside the book's designated unbeliever.

**The beat that breaks Eli's chart open:** the last fish-hook is three weeks old, in Rhys's handwriting — meaning the folklore's most recent data point comes from the man who mocks it loudest.

**With Eli:** half the book is the two of them protecting each other from one conversation. When it finally happens it should be short, concrete, and nobody cries on the page.

**Stake in the ground:** Rhys never converts to belief either — he converts to *admitting what he was already doing*. The book's two parents show the two honest ways to keep a light without believing in it.
