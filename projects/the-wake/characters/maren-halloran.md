# Maren Halloran — 19, Eli's sister, decommissioning tech

**The one who got out — by joining the thing that's coming for the light.** Junior tech on the contractor's circuit: the good job, the one every station kid is supposed to want, the one the family toasted when she got it. She arrives in chapter 3 with the advance crew that will automate her own family's light, wearing the patch, and nobody at the kitchen table is rude about it, which is so much worse than if they were.

**Why she left (her case, and it's a real one):** the keeper life is beautiful and it is also a closed loop — six generations deep, no way up, no way out, and the law that keeps families on lights also keeps kids like her from everything else. Maren is what the theme looks like from outside: she'll tell you faithfulness is just what trapped people call the trap. She is not wrong. The book never pretends she is.

**The turn:** Eli shows her the chart expecting big-sister mockery. She debunks it her way — modern gear, company math, proper statistics — as a favor, to let him down properly. The numbers don't cooperate. Act I ends with her quiet, and the pact: prove it before the archive ships. Her contractor access + his archive literacy = the instrument, working.

**The cost (hers to carry in Act II):** helping means stealing baseline time from her employer and betting the career she left home for on her little brother's chart of Gran's superstition. Maren has the most to lose of anyone in Book 1.

**Stake in the ground:** she never quits the company on the page in Book 1. The easy version of her arc is resignation-as-redemption; the committed version is harder — she stays inside, conflicted, useful, and unforgiven by herself either way. Book 2 material.
