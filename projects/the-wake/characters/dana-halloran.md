# Dana Halloran — Mum, chief keeper, sixth generation

**The light is hers.** Edda's daughter. Runs the watch like liturgy: exact, unsentimental, no margin-talk on duty. She is NOT a believer in the folklore — that's the point. Dana's faith is in the *discipline* itself: you keep the light because keeping is what we are, not because the fish-hooks mean anything. Which makes her the last person in the family to accept the chart — and the most devastating convert when she does, because she converts on evidence, in front of her children, and says so plainly.

**The decommission:** she signed the handover papers herself rather than make the company force it — her version of protecting the family, and the decision Eli can't forgive for most of the book. Her reason, revealed late and concrete: something in the terms she traded for, quietly, that the kids don't know about. (What exactly — committed in Act II drafting.)

**With Rhys:** she knows about the pen. Has always known. Says nothing, because the marks are his grief for Edda and grief gets the margin. The reveal that she knew reframes half the book's silences.

**Stake in the ground:** Dana never becomes warm. She becomes *legible*. The reader's arc is learning to read her the way keepers read a margin.
