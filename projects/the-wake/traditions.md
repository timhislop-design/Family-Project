# The Wake — Keeper Traditions (Claude's commit — Tim's red pen welcome; Abby-safe)

House rule inherited from The Deep and Firmament: every tradition is warm on the page and load-bearing underneath. No decoration.

## The light names the family

A keeper who marries in takes the light's name — the light doesn't take theirs. Rhys became a Halloran the day he signed the watch book; his old surname appears exactly once in the novel, somewhere quiet. Underneath: continuity of the *record* outranks continuity of blood — the same-name, same-hand, same-format log is (unknown to everyone) what makes the archive an instrument. The tradition is the telescope.

## The Quiet Hour

Once a year, every crewed light in the chain goes radio-silent for one hour — the hour the automated relay let the distress call rot. Keepers stand the watch in silence; kids learn to hate it, then to love it, in that order. Underneath: the law written in blood, renewed annually without a word of explanation. Also the only hour a year the whole chain's instruments listen without talking — which will matter in Act III.

## First Light

A keeper kid's first solo watch. The whole family stays awake pretending to be asleep. The kid signs the log alone for the first time; the entry is traditionally terrible and traditionally never corrected. Underneath: the log is sacred *because* it's human — errors, jokes, grief and all. Gran's first entry, age eleven, is quoted somewhere in Act II and it is not good, and it is perfect. Eli's First Light lands months before Light-Out, and nobody says the obvious thing.

## Last Watch

When a light is decommissioned, the final crewed watch is stood by the whole family together, and the last log entry is written by the youngest keeper — the one losing the most future. Then the book is closed, wrapped, and *kept by the family* — the only volume the company never gets. Underneath: Book 1's entire third act stands on this stage. The company can take the light; the log goes home. Which means the company was never going to get the truth — only the family could ever have carried it.

## The margin

Official columns are for the company. The margin is for the keeper. Weather that isn't weather, jokes, births, the fish-hooks. Underneath: the whole novel. The truth of the solar system lives in the margins of a bureaucratic document — the place where humans wrote down what they weren't asked to.
