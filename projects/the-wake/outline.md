Spoiler-light: this file stays readable for Abby. The sealed answers live in `canon-vault.md`.

## The clock (Book 1 rides the decommission schedule)

The Deep rides a school year; The Wake rides a countdown: **nine months from handover notice to Light-Out** — the scheduled night the Halloran Light goes automated and the family ships in-system. Act I = the notice and the discovery. Act II = proving it while the archive disappears crate by crate. Act III = Last Watch. The book ends the night the light is supposed to die.

## The family (committed)

The keeper line runs through **Mum — Dana Halloran**, chief keeper, sixth generation. **Dad — Rhys Halloran — married in and took the name**, because that's the tradition: *the light names the family* (see `traditions.md`). Rhys publicly half-mocks the folklore of his dead mother-in-law **Edda** (Gran) — and has been secretly keeping her margin-marks going for years. **Maren Halloran, 19**, Eli's older sister, took the good job every station kid dreams of — junior tech on the decommissioning circuit. She arrives with the advance crew that will shut down her own family's light. Nobody in this family is wrong. That's the engine.

## Act I — "Class C" (Ch. 1–6)

1. **Class C.** The clay: stickers, the scanning, Gran's fish-hooks, the joke chart that comes out straight, the last point in Dad's handwriting. Ends: Eli tells no one, steals one logbook off the disposal crate.
2. **The Halloran Light.** Station life, warm and specific. Dana's watch discipline. The nine-month clock lands. The Quiet Hour observed (the annual silence for the lost distress call — the law written in blood, planted without ever being explained).
3. **Maren.** The sister arrives wearing the contractor's patch. Banter over comms turning careful at the kitchen table. Eli almost shows her the chart. Doesn't.
4. **First Light.** Eli's first solo watch — the tradition every keeper kid waits for, landing months before the light dies, which everyone pretends isn't bitter. During his watch: a crest. The light answers early *under his hands*. Not folklore now. His.
5. **The Fish-Hook.** Eli circles Dad obliquely. Discovers the secret continuation — not by confession but by a concrete thing (Gran's pen, refilled, in Rhys's coat). Two people who can't say what they both know.
6. **Best Effort, Low Priority.** First crates actually leave. Eli breaks and shows Maren the chart. She runs it her way — modern instruments, company math — expecting to debunk her little brother. Goes quiet instead. Act I ends on the two of them making the pact: prove it before the archive's gone.

## Act II — "The Instrument" (sketch, chapters to be committed after Tim's Act I pass)

The siblings work both sides — Eli in the paper archive, Maren stealing baseline time on company gear. The problem the whole act turns on: the proof *requires* the century of logs, because only the long baseline shows the pattern (this is why the archive matters cosmically, not just sentimentally). The company assessor arrives — humane, reasonable, completely convinced the family is romanticizing junk; the antagonist is the schedule, never a villain. Family fracture: Dana finds out what her kids are doing; the Rhys secret surfaces. Midpoint: the line gets an endpoint — it points *at* somewhere. Act ends: archive seizure moved up; the instrument is about to be landfilled.

## Act III — "Last Watch" (sketch)

Light-Out night is a Last Watch ceremony (see `traditions.md`) — and the math says a crest arrives the same week. Everything converges on one watch, one log entry, one family deciding whether to hand over the keys. The light is kept lit past its death date; the final page of Book 1 is a logbook page. What exactly it records: committed after Act II is written — but the book's last line is Eli's first official entry as a keeper of a light that officially doesn't exist.

## The theme, enforced

Witness. Every act must contain at least one scene where *recording something faithfully* changes what happens — and no scene where a chase does. If it can't be done with a log entry, a chart, or a kept light, it doesn't belong in this book.
