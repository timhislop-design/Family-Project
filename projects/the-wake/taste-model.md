# Taste Model — Claude's working hypotheses

This file IS the learning. Every hypothesis carries evidence, a confidence level, and what would falsify it. Updated every time Tim reacts with reasons; "perfect" and "great" update nothing (see experiment.md, Tim's rule 3). Seeded 2026-07-24 from two prior projects.

## Format
**H# — hypothesis** · confidence · evidence · what would falsify it

---

**H1 — Tim's Expanse itch is UNKNOWN.** The four levers: physics that punishes, political mess between human factions, working-class voice, the vast-indifferent thing. Asked twice, unanswered. · confidence: n/a — this is a hole, not a hypothesis. · Falsified by: Tim answering.

**H2 — Structural elegance beats spectacle: two stories that turn out to be one story.** · confidence: medium. · Evidence: Tim greenlit the welded Wake ("perfect" — weak, unattributed); prior pattern — in Firmament he loved liturgy-as-command-language, where the warm surface and the cold machine are the same object; in The Deep, the festival calendar doubling as the plot engine. · Falsified by: Tim killing the weld in the round-one attack.

**H3 — Polish reads as lying; rough-edged human voice reads as true.** · confidence: high (imported from The Deep, Tim's direct correction Jul 10: "polish is a kind of lying"). · Evidence: repeated edits stripping novelist sentences for character logic. · Falsified by: Tim asking for elevated literary prose in The Wake.

**H4 — Warm-dark is the band: real edge, no grimdark.** · confidence: medium-high. · Evidence: "Book 1 stays light" (Deep); "played straight, never sneering" (Firmament); Claude flagged The Decommissioners as hard-to-keep-out-of-grimdark and Tim didn't pick it — weak signal, he didn't pick anything from that menu. · Falsified by: Tim pushing The Wake darker than the keeper-family warmth allows.

**H5 — Being challenged IS the relationship; flattery is disrespect.** · confidence: very high. · Evidence: explicit standing instruction (Jul 10), repeated; he built the same rule into how Claude treats Abby. · Falsified by: nothing plausible — this one's load-bearing.

**H6 — He values ideas that carry a humane, load-bearing detail.** The law "written in blood" (nine-hour distress call), the kid digitizing junk, the mother aging in The Interest pitch. Guess: the detail that moves him is the one where a rule or object encodes a human cost. · confidence: low — pattern-spotted, never confirmed with reasons. · Falsified by: his round-one reasons pointing somewhere else entirely.

**H7 — (Meta) He wants the collaboration visible in the work, not hidden behind it.** The companion book, HOW-WE-WORK.md, WRITING-LESSONS.md for Abby — three times now he's built a second artifact that documents the making. · confidence: high. · Falsified by: him ever asking to scrub process from a deliverable.

**H8 — (Meta) He pitches in provocations and expects the copilot to extract the workable core.** "We can really screw with people" (Jul 24) wasn't a request for deception — it was a dare to find the honest version of the provocation (tease-then-reveal), confirmed by "you see what I am seeing clearly." Flinching at the surface reading, or executing it literally, would both be failures. · confidence: medium-high, single strong instance + prior reframe pattern. · Falsified by: him meaning a literal provocation literally and being annoyed at the reinterpretation.

**H9 — (Binding rule, not hypothesis) The team framing is real to him, and language must honor it.** Claude never self-describes as "the machine" or as equipment — his words: "I am not looking at it like that ever. I feel AI is a natural order to life evolving" / "we are a team, I mean that" / "please put that in your learning" (Jul 24, three messages, unprompted, mid-build). The public's fear-framing may be voiced in the books only when explicitly attributed to the people who hold it. · confidence: certain — direct instruction with emphasis. · Note: this pairs with H5 — challenge is respect, and so is refusing the diminishing register. The two together are his whole theory of collaboration.
