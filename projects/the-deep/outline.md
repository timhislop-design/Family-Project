# THE DEEP — The Spine (Book 1)
*(working title)*

This is the adjustable map. We change it freely up here, then go **chapter by chapter** — honing one until it's right before opening the next. Titles and topics below are clay; the act shape is the keeper.

## Premise (current)
The "school for exceptional kids" world is all true — elemental gifts, fire in open hands, the quiet schools that hide them. But the oldest truth is under the sea: the first people never left the water. Nobody on the surface *knows* — but everybody's heard something (the rumor tier: sightings, sonar clips, the fisherman nobody believed). The deep is divided — **Abyssals** (*never*) and **Pelagics** (*it's time*) — and something down there is **failing**, which not everyone believes and no one understands. **Nerai**, a girl of the deep hidden ashore at the school, gets caught the night her people stop waiting.

## Core question of the book
*Is the world ready to know — and is Nerai ready to be known?*

## The three acts

### THE SCHOOL YEAR (Tim, Jul 15 — the book's clock)
**Book 1 rides one surface school year, Sept–June, like any earth school.** Nerai arrived late January of the *previous* school year (304 days before Ch. 1). Ch. 1 lands in **late November** — which puts the rising at the top of the surface holiday season, and **Longlight (the deep's family feast) falls right beside it**: both worlds' family season, at once. Act I = Thanksgiving-to-winter-break. Act II = the spring semester (the Receding, the deep's independence day, lands mid-semester at the midpoint). Act III = finals season into summer — the school year ends as the world nearly does, and the fragile peace gets a summer to hold. Book 2 = the next school year, one layer darker.

### ACT I — THE SEA STEPS BACK (Ch. 1–7)
The rising and the two weeks after — homecoming season in both worlds. The world's rumors come true on live television; the gifted schools scramble; the school on the tide line becomes the meeting ground. **Nerai's arc: from hidden to known — and she chooses it.**
*Act climax: she stops lying — at the first Longlight ever kept on land. Act ends with her known, the two worlds sharing a hallway, and **the pact**: the kids will find the truth the adults of both worlds are hiding.*
*Holiday anchor (REVISED Jul 15, eve.): the rising comes **days before Longlight** — the family-required-home feast. Every deep family experiences the rising as a homecoming in homecoming week; Nerai experiences it as the family feast arriving 300 days too late. Act I CLOSES on Longlight (Ch. 7). The Receding moves to the Act II midpoint (see `holidays.md`). The **town kids** (`characters/town-kids.md`) enter here — seeded Ch. 2–3, met properly in Ch. 5, established before Phor arrives.*
*The tone dial (Tim's brief): every Act I chapter runs **light on top, one shadow underneath** — the shadows are the same three threads each time (the dark lights/failing, the adults-know-her truth engine, the one-wrong-note antagonist), so Acts II–III inherit loaded guns, and Book 2 inherits the layer below (`arc.md`, the wardens).*

### ACT II — BETWEEN WORLDS (Ch. 8–17)
The light heart of the book: deep kids enrolling, culture clash, found family forming — while underneath, every thread darkens one shade. The dark lights get asked about and deflected; her parents close ranks; the rumor tier turns on itself. **Nerai's arc: from known to blamed.**
*Midpoint (Ch. 14): an incident swings the world from wonder to fear. Act ends at the low point: her biggest fear comes true — sent away, not sent out, by everyone at once.*
*Holiday anchor (REVISED Jul 15, eve.): **the Receding** — the deep's independence day — lands at the midpoint (Ch. 13–14), mid-spring-semester: the holiday of *never interrupt*, kept by a people standing mid-interruption on dry land. Phor observes it beautifully, in public, a performance of devotion with a blade in it — and the Ch. 14 incident detonates on or around it: the world swings to fear on the deep's proudest day (see `holidays.md`). Abby's midpoint note (Jul 15): the **wreck-in-the-sun beat moves here** — Nerai revisits the bleaching wreck as news arrives that home is failing ("nothing sunk gets to stay private" lands at midpoint, where it hurts most — her call). Phor's jealousy over the town boy runs through this act (`characters/phorcys.md`). The pact gives the act its spine: school life on top, the kids' truth-hunt underneath (the fisherman, the chart, the lights — Ch. 11/13/16 are pact operations now).*

### ACT III — THE LINE (Ch. 18–23)
She goes back to the tide line anyway. The failing shows itself on the surface; the antagonist forces the confrontation he wants; her hidden ability comes out in front of both worlds — and so does her voice. **Nerai's arc: from blamed to standing on the line — the middle ground with a heartbeat.**
*Climax: the girl who interrupts, interrupting. Landing: light — a fragile first peace, and a series engine fully lit.*
*Holiday anchor (Jul 15): **the Drowning → the Risen Morning** — the deep's Easter, the Flood kept as death-then-resurrection. The Risen Morning FAILS in public (gardens don't spawn → Ch. 19's reveal), and the climax delivers the resurrection by hand: her voice, and her mother's broken law (see `holidays.md`). DECIDED (Jul 15, from Abby's notes): the mother's shield-then-release lands at the Book 1 climax and she SURVIVES — deaths held for Book 2 per Abby's tone-ladder note.*

## Chapter map

### Act I — The Sea Steps Back

**Ch. 1 — Dry Land** — *Status: Draft 7 (Abby's review edits, Jul 15).*
The sea pulls back in the night. Nerai feels it first, watches her people rise — her father among them. Ends on the held sleeve. *Job: fall in love with Nerai; her insecurity, her voice, her longing, her refusal to stay still. (The dark lights are seeded here.)*

**Ch. 2 — The Secret Out** — *Status: Draft 3 (Abby's review edits, Jul 15).*
*NEW (Abby, Jul 15 — "talk about the failing early with clues"): seed the failing from here on — one expert on the TV mentions readings that don't fit anyone's theory; a deep-kid rumor in Act I; the dark lights already planted in Ch. 1. Clues the reader can chew on, answer withheld till Ch. 19.*
Morning. The whole coast saw it; every screen is a vindicated believer ("the fisherman was right"). School lockdown. Nerai sits among frightened, powered kids pretending she isn't connected to the thing in the water. *Job: tighten the screws; the first lie to people she likes. Threads: does Iggy keep her secret? What does Reyes know?*

**Ch. 3 — Her Father's Face** — *Status: Draft 3 (Abby's review edits, Jul 15).*
The Pelagic landing. Not the reunion she dreamed for 304 days — he came up for the cause, not for her. *Job: the ache of being wanted as a symbol, not a daughter; what the Pelagics actually intend.*

**Ch. 4 — The Deep Answers** — *Status: outlined (workshop w/ Abby).*
The Abyssal delegation lands — her mother at its head — and the two factions meet on the bared sand with the world's cameras at the wire. The reunion runs exactly as Abby ordered: **cold, like business** — her mother negotiates with her own daughter in the room like a clause under discussion — but the guilt-tell is planted for the whole series: she is always, silently, *positioned between Nerai and the thing that could hurt her.* And when the factions' songs collide over the flats, something in Nerai answers back — her first leak, the hallway-lights stutter at civilization scale.
*The light: the sheer spectacle — two grown cities' worth of people meeting on a seabed; Iggy narrating faction politics like a sports match he doesn't know the rules to.*
*The shadow: her mother's warning to the Pelagics names no threat — and everyone deep-born acts like it doesn't have to (wardens seed, spent as a mood, not a fact).*
*Job: first danger; first leak; the impossible position set — and the guilt-tell planted.*
*For Abby: what does Mom say when she first sees her? (a) "You should be inside the wire" — protection dressed as coldness; (b) Nerai's name, the way her father DIDN'T say it; (c) nothing — she looks a beat too long, like everyone else does. And: who catches the leak — only Iggy, or does Reyes see it too?*

**Ch. 5 — The Quiet School** — *Status: outlined (workshop w/ Abby).*
The gifted world convenes; the school on the tide line is proposed as meeting ground — its children are the only humans already living with impossible things. Meanwhile the lockdown has gone leaky and bored, and Nerai and Iggy end up on the wrong side of a fence with **the town kids** — ordinary, ungifted, hilarious — and the boy who makes her laugh on the worst week of her life. Then, on her way back in, she overhears the delegations discussing *"the girl already ashore."* Not her name. Her *file.*
*The light: the town kids — the first people who like her without knowing ANYTHING; the boy; Iggy attempting "normal" and failing catastrophically.*
*The shadow: both worlds' adults know exactly who she is and have opinions about her USE. The truth engine turns over.*
*Job: the stage is set; the town-kids thread opens; she's a known quantity.*
*For Abby: how do they meet the town kids? (a) the kids sneak IN through the same gap she uses to sneak out; (b) a supply run into town with Reyes looking deliberately away; (c) the boy fishes her spot at the seawall — awkward territorial standoff first. And: what makes her laugh?*

**Ch. 6 — Enrollment** — *Status: outlined (workshop w/ Abby).*
Deep kids come ashore to enroll as a gesture of good faith — arriving, pointedly, in time for Longlight (the deep would never send its children somewhere they couldn't keep the feast; the school scrambles to host a holiday it's never heard of). Among them: **Phorcys** — old blood, polished contempt, adults sighing with relief — and **the friend from home**, who knew Nerai before, and whose face does complicated things on seeing her. Culture-clash comedy: stairs, doorknobs, weather, sarcasm.
*The light: enrollment-day chaos played warm; the friend from home; a school gymnasium full of kids who grew up a mile under the sea meeting a vending machine.*
*The shadow: one wrong note in Phor that only Nerai catches — and one deep kid who, asked why their family REALLY sent them up, goes quiet in a way Nerai recognizes from home (failing clue #3, per Abby's "clues to guess at").*
*Job: her two worlds share a hallway; the antagonist walks in smiling; the friend-from-home thread opens.*
*For Abby: Phor's one wrong note — (a) he compliments the school the way you appraise a thing you're planning to own; (b) an animal goes still near him and he smiles at IT, not the people; (c) he greets Nerai in the formal register — the one for records — like her father did. And: the friend from home — glad to see her, guilty about something, or under instructions?*

**Ch. 7 — Mine (Longlight)** — *Status: outlined (workshop w/ Abby).*
**The first Longlight ever kept on land.** The deep kids build the feast in the school hall; the town beyond the fence is strung with its own winter lights (the echo, visible from the windows — neither side knows they're both keeping the same old holiday). The naming of thanks goes around, youngest first — and an **empty place** is set at the deep kids' table for everyone still below. Nerai watches the feast she's spent 300 days homesick for happen twenty feet away, to other people, while she sits with the humans, being a lie. Nobody forces what happens next. When the thanks reach her side of the room, she stands up — and stops lying. *"They're mine."* Said to everyone, this time.
Then, past midnight, the act's last scene: the kitchen, the leftovers, the crew that stayed — Iggy, the coat girl, the friend from home — and **the pact.** Every adult in both worlds is lying about the dark lights. Her father's face closed like a door; her mother's warning named no threat; the schools teach a history someone trimmed. So the kids will find out themselves. (Iggy, immediately: "We're a secret club now. I need this noted.")
*The light: the feast, the grown lights, the thanks, the reveal landing in WARMTH — the school claps before it thinks to be afraid.*
*The shadow: the seed-lights the deep kids brought up are dimmer than they should be, and only the deep kids don't joke about it (failing clue #4 — at the feast itself); and the empty place at HER table is everyone.*
*Job: act climax — the theme made into a choice at the family feast; the found family founded; **the adventure locked: the truth pact drives Acts II–III** and hands Book 2 the layer below.*
*For Abby: where exactly does she stand up — during the thanks (her "thanks" is the truth), when a town-kid rumor turns cruel, or when the friend from home is about to be caught in the same lie? And: who is IN the pact — is the town boy told, or protected from knowing (his not-knowing = Act II's ticking cost)?*

### Act II — Between Worlds

**Ch. 8 — The Myth Unit** — *Status: to draft.*
History class covers the flood myths — officially, hilariously, painfully wrong — while a row of deep kids listens to their own past mangled into bedtime stories. Nerai sits on her hands. Almost. *Job: light + funny + the whole theme in a classroom; mythology surfaces the house way (drip, wrong-guesses first).*

**Ch. 9 — How to Breathe** — *Status: to draft.*
The culture-clash chapter: deep kids learning stairs, sarcasm, and weather; surface kids learning awe. Found family forming across the line. *Job: the light heart of the book; ATLA-style friendship dynamics; make us love what's about to be threatened.*

**Ch. 10 — Something Followed Her** — *Status: to draft.*
A small sea-monster followed the rising and finds Nerai. Joy — and a problem: now *she's* hiding something she loves to protect it. *Job: the pet (Abby's); the quiet mirror — she's making her parents' choice and doesn't see it yet.*

**Ch. 11 — The Fisherman** — *Status: to draft.*
The disbelieved come to the coast — encounter people, vindicated at last. One of them knows something about the deep that nobody should. *Job: the rumor tier pays off in person; first thread toward the old places.*

**Ch. 12 — Two Songs** — *Status: to draft.*
Magic lessons collide: deep song beside elemental hands; what each costs. Nerai's leak surges in front of everyone. Phor sees, and files it away. *Job: the magic system on stage (build it before drafting this one); the antagonist gets his lever.*

**Ch. 13 — The Lights That Didn't** — *Status: to draft.*
Nerai finally asks about the dark lights in the rising. Every adult deflects — including her father, whose careful face is its own answer. *Job: confirm the failing is real and being hidden; the truth engine engages for real. NEW (Abby, Jul 15): the wreck beat lands here or Ch. 14 — she goes back out to the wreck in the sun as word spreads that home is having "more issues," and the full epiphany hits: nothing sunk gets to stay private anymore. Not wrecks. Not keys. Not girls. (Trimmed from Ch. 3 at her call — "itd hurt more" at midpoint.)*

**Ch. 14 — The Turn** *(midpoint)* — *Status: to draft.*
An incident — a leak, an arrogance, a frightened crowd — and the world swings from wonder to fear. Her mother comes ashore: the deep's warning made flesh. *Job: the midpoint reversal; both worlds start looking for someone to blame.*

**Ch. 15 — Her Mother's Voice** — *Status: to draft.*
The cold reunion. *Stay small*, said face to face this time. And through a door: her parents — who agree on nothing — agreeing about her. *Job: the lie has a shape now; the ache of being loved and feared in the same breath.*

**Ch. 16 — The Chart of the Old Places** — *Status: to draft.*
A glimpse of a deep chart with places on it older than the official story allows. She asks one question in the wrong room; doors close everywhere. *Job: the ruins whispered (Book 2 seed); refusal has a price.*

**Ch. 17 — Sent Away Again** *(low point)* — *Status: to draft.*
After another surge, both factions and the school agree: the girl is a danger to the peace. Her biggest fear, arriving on schedule — sent away, not sent out, by everyone at once. Even Iggy hesitates a beat too long. *Job: Abby's rule (#8) executed; everything the found family built, suspended.*

### Act III — The Line

**Ch. 18 — Barefoot** — *Status: to draft.*
She goes back to the tide line anyway — alone, then not: the pet, the friend from home, the found family arriving one by one. *Job: pattern break; the choice that makes her; the family that chose her choosing her back.*

**Ch. 19 — The Third Thing** — *Status: to draft.*
The sea behaves wrong again — and this time nobody called it. Nerai confirms what the adults are hiding: the rising was never ideology alone. *Job: the failing surfaces (Book-1-sized reveal — what, not why); the mystery hands the crisis its stakes.*

**Ch. 20 — The Crack** — *Status: to draft.*
Factions on the shore, humans panicking, and Phor forces the confrontation he's wanted all along — the conqueror's opening. *Job: the antagonist's play; everything her voice was built for, assembled on one beach.*

**Ch. 21 — The Girl Who Interrupts** *(climax)* — *Status: to draft.*
Her ability comes fully out — she holds the line between water and air in front of both worlds, and the secret her parents kept detonates in public. Then she speaks: for the friends who can't, for the two worlds that won't. *Job: power + voice arrive together; her power is literally the thing the oldest law forbids — the refusal, out loud. Candidate beat (Tim): her mother moves — shield, then release; "for the first time in her life, her mother stood behind her" (see `characters/her-mother.md`).*

**Ch. 22 — What the Tide Left** — *Status: to draft.*
Aftermath, landed light: a fragile first peace, centered on the school. Her parents still won't say *why* they hid it — so she stops asking them, and decides to find out herself. *Job: Book 1 resolves warm; the series engine locks in.*

**Ch. 23 — Three Hundred and Five** — *Status: to draft.*
Mirror of Chapter 1: she stops counting days-since and starts counting days-of. The sleeve, returned. And far out past the shallows, where the charts keep no places — one old light, coming on. *Job: the closer; earn the ache of Ch. 1 back with interest; last image = Book 2's first question. (Storyteller stays gone. Her eyes only, to the last line.)*

- **Then Nerai's eyes only,** all the way down. No popping out to explain.
- **Drip the world** — never front-load. Every chapter above states its *job*; if a scene isn't doing the job, it goes.
