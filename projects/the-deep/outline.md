# THE DEEP — The Spine (Book 1)
*(working title)*

This is the adjustable map. We change it freely up here, then go **chapter by chapter** — honing one until it's right before opening the next. Titles and topics below are clay; the act shape is the keeper.

## Premise (current)
The "school for exceptional kids" world is all true — elemental gifts, fire in open hands, the quiet schools that hide them. But the oldest truth is under the sea: the first people never left the water. Nobody on the surface *knows* — but everybody's heard something (the rumor tier: sightings, sonar clips, the fisherman nobody believed). The deep is divided — **Abyssals** (*never*) and **Pelagics** (*it's time*) — and something down there is **failing**, which not everyone believes and no one understands. **Nerai**, a girl of the deep hidden ashore at the school, gets caught the night her people stop waiting.

## Core question of the book
*Is the world ready to know — and is Nerai ready to be known?*

## The three acts

### THE SCHOOL YEAR (Tim, Jul 15 — the book's clock)
**Book 1 rides one surface school year, Sept–June, like any earth school.** Nerai arrived late January of the *previous* school year (304 days before Ch. 1). Ch. 1 lands in **late November** — which puts the rising at the top of the surface holiday season, and **Longlight (the deep's family feast) falls right beside it**: both worlds' family season, at once. Act I = Thanksgiving-to-winter-break. Act II = the spring semester (the Receding, the deep's independence day, lands mid-semester at the midpoint). Act III = finals season into summer — the school year ends as the world nearly does, and the fragile peace gets a summer to hold. Book 2 = the next school year, one layer darker.

### ACT I — THE SEA STEPS BACK (Ch. 1–7)
The rising and the two weeks after — homecoming season in both worlds. The world's rumors come true on live television; the gifted schools scramble; the school on the tide line becomes the meeting ground. **Nerai's arc: from hidden to known — and she chooses it.**
*Act climax: she stops lying — at the first Longlight ever kept on land. Act ends with her known, the two worlds sharing a hallway, and **the pact**: the kids will find the truth the adults of both worlds are hiding.*
*Holiday anchor (REVISED Jul 15, eve.): the rising comes **days before Longlight** — the family-required-home feast. Every deep family experiences the rising as a homecoming in homecoming week; Nerai experiences it as the family feast arriving 300 days too late. Act I CLOSES on Longlight (Ch. 7). The Receding moves to the Act II midpoint (see `holidays.md`). The **town kids** (`characters/town-kids.md`) enter here — seeded Ch. 2–3, met properly in Ch. 5, established before Phor arrives.*
*The tone dial (Tim's brief): every Act I chapter runs **light on top, one shadow underneath** — the shadows are the same three threads each time (the dark lights/failing, the adults-know-her truth engine, the one-wrong-note antagonist), so Acts II–III inherit loaded guns, and Book 2 inherits the layer below (`arc.md`, the wardens).*

### ACT II — BETWEEN WORLDS (Ch. 8–17)
The light heart of the book: deep kids enrolling, culture clash, found family forming — while underneath, every thread darkens one shade. The dark lights get asked about and deflected; her parents close ranks; the rumor tier turns on itself. **Nerai's arc: from known to blamed.**
*Midpoint (Ch. 14): an incident swings the world from wonder to fear. Act ends at the low point: her biggest fear comes true — sent away, not sent out, by everyone at once.*
*Holiday anchor (REVISED Jul 15, eve.): **the Receding** — the deep's independence day — lands at the midpoint (Ch. 13–14), mid-spring-semester: the holiday of *never interrupt*, kept by a people standing mid-interruption on dry land. Phor observes it beautifully, in public, a performance of devotion with a blade in it — and the Ch. 14 incident detonates on or around it: the world swings to fear on the deep's proudest day (see `holidays.md`). Abby's midpoint note (Jul 15): the **wreck-in-the-sun beat moves here** — Nerai revisits the bleaching wreck as news arrives that home is failing ("nothing sunk gets to stay private" lands at midpoint, where it hurts most — her call). Phor's jealousy over the town boy runs through this act (`characters/phorcys.md`). The pact gives the act its spine: school life on top, the kids' truth-hunt underneath (the fisherman, the chart, the lights — Ch. 11/13/16 are pact operations now).*

### ACT III — THE LINE (Ch. 18–23)
She goes back to the tide line anyway. The failing shows itself on the surface; the antagonist forces the confrontation he wants; her hidden ability comes out in front of both worlds — and so does her voice. **Nerai's arc: from blamed to standing on the line — the middle ground with a heartbeat.**
*Climax: the girl who interrupts, interrupting. Landing: light — a fragile first peace, and a series engine fully lit.*
*Holiday anchor (Jul 15): **the Drowning → the Risen Morning** — the deep's Easter, the Flood kept as death-then-resurrection. The Risen Morning FAILS in public (gardens don't spawn → Ch. 19's reveal), and the climax delivers the resurrection by hand: her voice, and her mother's broken law (see `holidays.md`). DECIDED (Jul 15, from Abby's notes): the mother's shield-then-release lands at the Book 1 climax and she SURVIVES — deaths held for Book 2 per Abby's tone-ladder note.*

And: the friend from home — glad to see her, guilty about something, or under instructions?*

**Ch. 7 — Mine (Longlight)** — *Status: Draft 1 (Jul 15, 2026).*
**The first Longlight ever kept on land.** The deep kids build the feast in the school hall; the town beyond the fence is strung with its own winter lights (the echo, visible from the windows — neither side knows they're both keeping the same old holiday). The naming of thanks goes around, youngest first — and an **empty place** is set at the deep kids' table for everyone still below. Nerai watches the feast she's spent 300 days homesick for happen twenty feet away, to other people, while she sits with the humans, being a lie. Nobody forces what happens next. When the thanks reach her side of the room, she stands up — and stops lying. *"They're mine."* Said to everyone, this time.
Then, past midnight, the act's last scene: the kitchen, the leftovers, the crew that stayed — Iggy, the coat girl, the friend from home — and **the pact.** Every adult in both worlds is lying about the dark lights. Her father's face closed like a door; her mother's warning named no threat; the schools teach a history someone trimmed. So the kids will find out themselves. (Iggy, immediately: "We're a secret club now. I need this noted.")
*The light: the feast, the grown lights, the thanks, the reveal landing in WARMTH — the school claps before it thinks to be afraid.*
*The shadow: the seed-lights the deep kids brought up are dimmer than they should be, and only the deep kids don't joke about it (failing clue #4 — at the feast itself); and the empty place at HER table is everyone.*
*Job: act climax — the theme made into a choice at the family feast; the found family founded; **the adventure locked: the truth pact drives Acts II–III** and hands Book 2 the layer below.*
*For Abby: where exactly does she stand up — during the thanks (her "thanks" is the truth), when a town-kid rumor turns cruel, or when the friend from home is about to be caught in the same lie? And: who is IN the pact — is the town boy told, or protected from knowing (his not-knowing = Act II's ticking cost)?*
