# Her Father *(name: Abby's call — Nereus / Ea / Palaemon starters on his page)*
*(Shell — on the page since Chapter 3. Pelagic. Leads the surfacing.)*

## What we know from the chapters so far
He came up with the rising and grew a shell of light on the bared sand to negotiate from. He greeted his daughter after three hundred and five days by confirming her name like an item on a list — then presented her to the elders as proof: *"Three hundred days among them. Unharmed. Unfound."* When she asked the one real question — *did you come up for them, or for me?* — he gave her the line she'll be taking apart for weeks: **"Both answers are yes."** And when she asked why some of the city's lights stayed dark, his face closed like a door being pulled shut by somebody who doesn't want you to hear the latch.

## His job in the story
- **The cause wearing a father's face.** He isn't cold because he doesn't love her; he's a man who long ago decided the mission and the family were the same thing, and has never noticed they aren't.
- **Keeper of the first big secret** (with her mother — the one thing the enemy factions agree on): what Nerai is, hidden "to protect her."
- **Keeper of the second:** the failing. He knows what the dark lights mean. His deflections are Nerai's breadcrumb trail (Ch. 13).

## How he works on the page
- Everything managed. Nothing slams. Flickers cross his face and get *handled* — she reads him like weather because she had to learn to.
- Two registers: the formal one (for records) and the quiet one — and the quiet one appears exactly when it can do the most damage.
- He never lies to her outright. He answers different questions than the ones she asks. (Father and Phorcys share this trait — that should bother the reader eventually.)

## The wound he carries (for later books)
He was a doubter before she was born — privately sure the hidden way couldn't hold. Then his own daughter turned out to be the proof, and he learned he was braver about ideas than about her. "Both answers are yes" is the truest and most cowardly thing he's ever said, at the same time, and some part of him knows it.

## Open for Abby (yours)
His name (Nereus would make "Nerai" a lineage — and the Greeks got their truthful sea god from HER family; or Ea, the deep-god who broke ranks to warn the flood's survivor; or Palaemon). And the one memory of him being an actual dad — it should exist, and hurt a little.
