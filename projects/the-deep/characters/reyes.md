# Coach Reyes *(first name: Abby's call — "Jonah" starter on his page)*
*(Shell — on the page since Chapter 2. School staff. Knows more than the sentence he's saying.)*

## What we know from the chapters so far
Runs fitness, evacuation drills, and — apparently — the end of the world. The morning after the rising he briefed a gym full of terrified gifted kids in his warm-up-laps voice, said *when* instead of *if*, used the phrase *representatives of the parties involved* like a man reading from an agreement nobody else has seen, and then, holding the door, said "**Long night, Nerai**" to a girl who had told no one she'd been up.

## His job in the story
- **The adult who already knows** — something, at least. The school becomes the meeting ground (Ch. 5) suspiciously smoothly, and Reyes is the hinge the smoothness swings on.
- **The safe adult.** Book 1 is light, and Reyes is part of why: whatever he knows, he's on the kids' side, visibly, in a crisis where every other adult is performing calm. He tells the truth in a school built on a curated one — just not all of it.
- **Nerai's read-problem.** She can read every face in the book except two: Phor's (mask) and Reyes's (furniture). It bothers her more with Reyes.

## How he works on the page
- One register for everything: hydration, homework, first contact. The flatness is the disguise AND the comfort.
- He never asks Nerai a question he doesn't already know the answer to — she figures this out around Ch. 13 and starts listening to his questions very differently.
- Clipboard he never looks at. Whistle he never blows. Props, both. Somebody should eventually ask what his actual job is.

## What does he actually know? (to decide with Abby — starters)
1. **The keeper:** the quiet schools hold an old compact with the sea — one staffer per school is told. He's the one.
2. **The witness:** ex-navy diver; saw something years ago, wasn't believed, got recruited by the people who believe everyone.
3. **The gift:** his own strange thing is small and terrible for secrets — he always knows where everyone is. ("Long night, Nerai" wasn't observation. It was roll call.)
