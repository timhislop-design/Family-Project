# Her Mother *(name: Abby's call — Tiama / Doris / Nammu / Ceto starters on her page)*
*(Shell — rises at the end of Chapter 3; lands in Chapter 4. Abyssal. Brings the deep's answer.)*

## What we know so far
She holds weight among the Abyssals — the *never* faction, keepers of the old law. She gave Nerai the official reason for going ashore (*learn humans quietly, stay small*) and a goodbye with no arms in it. Her horn, rolling up from the trench at the end of Chapter 3, is older and colder than her husband's polite knock — and every light on his grown shell flickered when it sounded. Every child of the deep knows her voice the way you know winter.

## Her job in the story
- **The deep's warning made flesh.** Where the father is the argument for rising, she is the argument for the dark — and the book has to let her be RIGHT about some of it (humans do fear; the rumors did leak; the cost will come).
- **The other keeper of the secret.** She agrees with her husband about exactly one thing: hide what Nerai is. Her "stay small" was protection dressed as coldness — and Nerai has never once been able to tell the two apart, which is the tragedy of them.
- **The harder reunion.** Chapter 3 was ache; Chapter 4 (and Ch. 15, "Her Mother's Voice") is confrontation. She says the quiet part to Nerai's face.

## How she works on the page
- Fewer words than anyone in the book. Her silences are architecture.
- She watches Nerai the way the sentries did — except she's been doing it since birth, cataloguing what leaks, calculating exposure.
- The tell that she loves her: she is always, always positioned between Nerai and the thing that could hurt her. Nobody notices, including Nerai. (Same tell as the pet. That's deliberate — the two characters who guard her both do it standing still.)

## The climax turn — Mom comes to help (Tim's, Jul 10, 2026 — candidate beat, placement open)
At some climax — strongest candidate: Book 1, Ch. 20–21, when Phor forces the confrontation and Nerai's secret detonates in public — **her mother moves.** The leader of *never*, the keeper of the oldest law, the woman whose answer to everything has been *stay small* — breaks her own doctrine in front of both worlds to stand with her daughter.

Why it lands like a hammer: her tell has been on the page since Chapter 4 — she is always, silently, positioned between Nerai and the thing that could hurt her, and nobody ever notices. The climax is where the tell goes public. And it can turn twice in one beat:

1. **The shield (old instinct):** she steps between Nerai and the danger — protection finally visible, the Abyssal leader defending the girl her own faction wanted buried. A political earthquake in one stride.
2. **The release (the real help):** then she sees her daughter's voice come out clear — and does the thing she has never once done. She steps *aside.* Stops shielding. Lets Nerai be seen, full size. Because sometimes help is getting out of the way.

And then the line the whole series can hang a frame on: **for the first time in her life, her mother stood behind her.** Both meanings. At once.

Cost to explore (nothing is free): what does breaking the old law cost HER — her standing, her faction, her certainty? That cost could be a Book 2 engine.
