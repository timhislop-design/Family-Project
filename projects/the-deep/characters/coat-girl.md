# The Coat Girl *(name: Abby's call — starters on her page)*
*(Shell — first of the found family. On the page since Chapter 2; becomes central in Act II.)*

## What we know from the chapters so far
Two doors down from Nerai. Lent her a coat in October without being asked — and never mentioned it again, not once, which Nerai was quietly grateful for all winter. Her gift is small and kind: she can warm a cup of anything between her palms, which sounds useless until you're sad and she hands you cocoa. The morning after the rising, red-eyed, she asked Nerai the direct question ("Did you ever… see anything?") and got the lie — then warmed Nerai's cocoa anyway, unasked.

## Her job in the story
She is **the cost of the lie.** Nerai's easy "No" in Chapter 2 was told to the one person whose kindness never asked for anything back. So when the truth lands (Ch. 7), HER reaction is the one that matters — not the crowd's. She's also the first proof that found family starts before you notice it starting: the coat was Chapter Zero of a friendship neither of them has admitted to yet.

## How she works on the page
- Kindness with no announcement — she does the thing and never references it.
- Small gift, huge timing. The cocoa always arrives at the right moment; that's HER intelligence.
- She notices more than she says (she's the one who asked, after all).

## Abby's casting pitch — the twins (Jul 15, 2026 — hers, needs one decision)
Her idea, verbatim: *"what if this character could be related to the character who never gets lost like siblings? Twins? Then this characters gift could be that they can locate anything (maybe anything once lost), but they're always unable to find it and they get lost if they try. Therefore their sibling has to get those things for them. Could lead to a cool search scene later. Names of each could correspond to their gifts but I can't think of them right now."*

Why it's good: a paired gift with a built-in flaw (she can *know* where a lost thing is but never reach it; her twin can reach anything but never needed to look) — that's a found-family engine and a ready-made Act II/III search set piece. And "anything once lost" has series-sized echoes: lost keys, lost wrecks, lost cities, a lost girl.

**The one snag (flag for Abby, don't fix silently):** the coat girl already has a gift on the page — she warms cups between her palms (Ch. 1 & 2), and the house rule is one gift per kid. So either (a) the *finder twin* is a NEW character — the never-lost kid's actual twin — and she joins the found family alongside the coat girl, or (b) we revise Ch. 1–2 so the coat girl's gift was always the finding, and someone else warms the cocoa. Option (a) costs nothing on the page and gives us one more kid for the Ch. 9 roster. **Question is back to Abby on the site.**
