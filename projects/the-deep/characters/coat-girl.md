# The Coat Girl *(name: Abby's call — starters on her page)*
*(Shell — first of the found family. On the page since Chapter 2; becomes central in Act II.)*

## What we know from the chapters so far
Two doors down from Nerai. Lent her a coat in October without being asked — and never mentioned it again, not once, which Nerai was quietly grateful for all winter. Her gift is small and kind: she can warm a cup of anything between her palms, which sounds useless until you're sad and she hands you cocoa. The morning after the rising, red-eyed, she asked Nerai the direct question ("Did you ever… see anything?") and got the lie — then warmed Nerai's cocoa anyway, unasked.

## Her job in the story
She is **the cost of the lie.** Nerai's easy "No" in Chapter 2 was told to the one person whose kindness never asked for anything back. So when the truth lands (Ch. 7), HER reaction is the one that matters — not the crowd's. She's also the first proof that found family starts before you notice it starting: the coat was Chapter Zero of a friendship neither of them has admitted to yet.

## How she works on the page
- Kindness with no announcement — she does the thing and never references it.
- Small gift, huge timing. The cocoa always arrives at the right moment; that's HER intelligence.
- She notices more than she says (she's the one who asked, after all).
