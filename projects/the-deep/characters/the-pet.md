# The One That Followed — "The Pet"
*(Enters Chapter 10. Shell with strong canon — Abby names it and finishes its look. One thing is NOT negotiable, per Tim: it is genuinely, truly intelligent.)*

## What it is (working shape — Abby confirms)
A **leviathan runt.** The deep breeds leviathans as mounts — the great beasts humans half-remembered onto the edges of their maps. Bred for size, for power, for obedience. This one was born too small. Worthless. Scheduled to be culled from the line, probably, which is roughly when a small girl with too many questions started sneaking it food.

Here's the thing nobody understood: the breeding traded something away. Generations of selecting for *big* and *obedient* quietly bred out what the first great beasts actually had — and the runt got it all back. The old trait. The original one.

## The intelligence (CANON — Tim, Jul 10, 2026)
**It has real smarts. An actual mind.** Not clever-dog smart — *thinking* smart:

- **It solves problems** — latches, nets, locked doors, situations. Given time, it has never met a container that held it. It waits, watches, and does things in the right ORDER, which is the tell: that's planning, not instinct.
- **It understands the song** — and increasingly, surface speech. It obeys nothing, but it *understands* nearly everything, and the difference between those two facts is the whole animal.
- **It chose her.** Nerai never tamed it. You don't tame it. It evaluated her, at some point, by standards nobody knows, and decided. Then it crossed half an ocean, alone, following a rising it was never part of, to find one girl on a strange shore. Loyalty at that scale isn't a pet's. It's a *decision,* renewed daily.
- **It can say no.** This matters more than anything: Phorcys's song commands grown and bred things — it's a leash. A leash only works on a mind that can't refuse. **The runt refuses.** His power slides off it like light off a wet rock, and it hated him before it ever surfaced, because it knew about his family's song the way you know about your family's old enemies. It is the one creature in the book his gift cannot touch. (Nobody reads this sign. They should.)
- **It knows things.** It reacts to the failing — goes still and wrong before the sea does. It's drawn toward the old places on no chart (the ruins). What it knows and how, we don't spend in Book 1. But when it stares at the dark water, it is not being a dog. It is *remembering,* or listening, and we don't know which yet.

**The guardrail:** it never talks, never gets cute-telepathic. Its mind shows only in behavior — in timing, in problem-solving, in choices that are a beat smarter than anyone expected. It should be funny sometimes and slightly frightening at least once. The reader should slowly realize the smallest character might be the oldest mind on the beach.

## What it WANTS (the motivation — a real one, because it has a real mind)
A creature this intelligent doesn't cross an ocean on affection alone. It has two motivations, one on top of the other:

**The near one (Book 1, on the page):** *Be where she is. Guard the only mind that ever asked its opinion.* Everyone else who ever looked at it saw a failed mount — a vehicle that came out wrong. Nerai looked at it and waited to see what it would DO, which is the only respect a mind like this counts. It isn't loyal the way a dog is loyal. It ran the numbers on every person it ever met, and she's the only one who passed. Its motivation is to keep that one good verdict alive — and it would do genuinely frightening things to protect it.

**The deep one (the series engine — dripped, never dumped):** the great beasts were never bred to be mounts. Before the Withdrawal, they were **partners** — companions of the first people, and the keepers of the line between the worlds. The shapes on the old human maps weren't monsters guarding the edge of the sea; they were sentries *holding* it. Twelve thousand years of breeding turned partners into vehicles and bred the purpose out — but the runt got the old mind back, and the old mind came with the old errand still in it: **guard the door between the worlds.** It crossed an ocean because the door is failing — and because, though nobody in the book knows it yet, the door has a heartbeat now. It didn't just follow the girl it loves. It reported for duty.

*(Those two motivations are secretly the same one. That's why it works.)*

## How Nerai learns it (the slow reveal — she figures X out over time)
She starts with the wrong answer and upgrades it book by book, the way she does everything:

1. **Book 1 (Ch. 10 on):** she thinks it followed her because it missed her. Warm, simple — and the reader gets little snags that don't fit: it positions itself between her and open water; it inspects the shore like it's *checking* something; it hates Phor with informed hatred.
2. **Book 1, late:** first crack — during the crisis she realizes it isn't following her around. It's *stationed.* It holds ground like it's holding a post, and it reacts to the failing before the sea does. She files it on the shelf.
3. **Book 2:** she learns the mounts' history — partners once, not vehicles — and starts asking the question that unlocks it: *what was the partnership FOR?* The old carvings, the map-serpents at the edges: not monsters. Sentries.
4. **Book 3+:** the full answer, at the worst possible time: the old errand is "guard the door between the worlds" — and the door is her. It didn't come back for her. It came back *to its post,* and its post is a girl. Whether that breaks her heart or steadies her is the scene's whole job — probably both.

*(Rule: every reveal recontextualizes an earlier cute moment. The Ch. 10 chapter of pure joy should reread completely differently after Book 3.)*

## What it is to Nerai
The one relationship with no factions in it. It never wanted her to be proof of anything, never feared what she was — it *liked* what she was, back when everyone else was going quiet around her. Leaving it behind is one of her real griefs (see her file: what she misses most). And now she's hiding something she loves to protect it — her parents' exact move, and she doesn't see the mirror yet. That mirror is Chapter 10's quiet job.

## How it works on the page
- Enters Ch. 10: something small following the rising, finding her. Joy first — the chapter earns the warmth before it earns the questions.
- Comedy through competence: it is better at things than it should be, deadpan, unimpressed.
- The Phor tell: every scene they share, it goes low and still and watches him. Once — exactly once in Book 1 — his song brushes it and *nothing happens,* and Phor's face does something nobody catches but Nerai.
- The mystery tell: it reacts to the failing before instruments, adults, or the sea itself. Used sparingly, it's the reader's early-warning system.
