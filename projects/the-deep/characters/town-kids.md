# The Town Kids *(new — Tim, Jul 15, 2026. Shell; names/casting with Abby)*
*(A small group of ordinary teens from the local town — no gifts, no school, no idea what the school on the hill actually is. Introduced in Act I, outside the school. One of the boys matters.)*

## Who they are
Three or four locals — fishing-town kids, the kind who've grown up half-ignoring the weird private school up the road. They meet Nerai and Iggy **outside the school walls** (Act I — candidate slots below), and they are the first people in the book who like Nerai without knowing a single thing about her. Not her gift, not her face-that's-almost-wrong, not the rumors. Just her.

## Why they matter (the job)
- **The only ungifted friends in the book.** Everyone else Nerai knows has a thing. The town kids have *nothing* — and around them, for the first time, neither does she. No passing, no protocol, no shelf. That's the point: with them she gets to find out who she is when nobody's measuring.
- **The boy.** One of them — she takes a liking to him, and he to her. Easy where Iggy is anxious, unimpressed where Phor is entitled — because he doesn't know there's anything to be impressed BY. Where this goes is open (the triangle in `../arc.md` just became a quadrilateral nobody in the book can see) — but in Book 1 it stays light, sweet, and doomed to complication the moment the worlds collide.
- **Phor's jealousy lever.** When Phor finds out, it's the insult of his life: an omen of the old blood preferring a land dweller with *nothing*. His jealousy is the crack the mask slips through — his hatred of the "earth people" shown in private, aimed at someone defenseless, long before the school sees it. (Full mechanism in `phorcys.md`.)
- **Humanity's stake, made personal.** When fear sweeps the town in Act II–III, it isn't an abstract crowd — it's *these* kids and their parents. And when Nerai's secret lands on them, their reaction is the ordinary world's verdict in miniature (pairs with the coat girl as "the cost of the lie" — hers is gifted, theirs is human).

## The dynamics
- **With Iggy:** he's never had ungifted friends either — the quiet schools don't allow it. Watching Iggy try to be normal (hide the flame, unlearn the reflexes) is comedy AND a mirror of Nerai's whole year. He's terrible at it. She's watched him be terrible at keeping secrets since Chapter 2.
- **With Nerai:** the boy notices her the way the sentries did — but what he recognizes is nothing supernatural: she watches the water like it owes her money, she never talks about home, she flinches when boats are mentioned. He reads her as *a kid with a story*, which is the most human read anyone's ever given her.
- **The secret pressure:** every scene with them, Nerai and Iggy are lying by omission — one more room where she's the only lie in it. When the truth lands, the town kids find out LAST. That should cost her.

## Where they enter (Act I — candidate slots, decide when honing)
