# The Friend From Home *(name: Abby's call — Nereid starters on their page)*
*(Shell — arrives Chapter 6 with the enrolled deep kids. Not yet on the page.)*

## Who they are
A deep-one kid who knew Nerai *before* — before the ability leaked, before the whispers got loud, before she was sent up. Which makes them the only person at the school who knew the girl, not the omen or the exhibit. When they walk in with the enrollment party, they bring the thing Nerai has gone three hundred days without: someone who remembers her laugh in the old language.

## Their job in the story
- **The mirror:** they are who Nerai would have been if she'd swallowed the story — they follow, defer, repeat the family's faction lines. Not weak; raised right, by the deep's lights. Watching Nerai refuse things costs them something to watch.
- **The pause:** the friendship didn't end. It got *interrupted* — she was sent up and nobody explained anything to anyone. The first conversation in Chapter 6 has a year of unsent messages in it.
- **The evidence:** through them we learn what the deep said about Nerai after she left. They know what the whispers were. Whether they repeat them is a character-defining choice.

## How they work on the page
- Formal where Nerai is blunt; they flinch when she questions elders — then look around to see who noticed.
- Small defections: they never argue with Nerai in public, but the cocoa-level kindnesses land where the faction wouldn't approve.
- Their arc (series): learning to have a voice — Nerai's whole friend-thesis is that her friends follow; this is the one who might stop.
