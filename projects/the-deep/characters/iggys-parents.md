# Iggy's Parents
*(Canon per Tim, Jul 10, 2026: unassuming, quirky — and the warmest room in the book. Names are Abby's call; starters below.)*

## Who they are
Two thoroughly ordinary people who produced a boy who carries fire, and reacted to that the way they react to everything: they set another place at the table. Nothing about them is impressive on paper. That's the point. In a book full of elders, factions, delegations, and ten-thousand-year secrets, Iggy's parents are the only adults with no agenda at all — and that makes their kitchen the most powerful room in the story.

They are **unassuming** — nobody at the school could pick them out of a crowd, and they like it that way. And they are **quirky** in the specific, lived-in way of people who stopped performing for anyone decades ago.

## The quirks (starters — Abby adjusts freely)
- **Dad** hums constantly, always the wrong song for the moment, at the wrong speed. Fixes things that aren't broken; owns one chair that is *his* chair; refers to the ocean, always, as "the water," like it's a neighbor he's known too long to be formal with.
- **Mom** talks back to the television and wins. Measures nothing when she cooks and it always comes out right. Keeps a drawer of objects that "might be important someday" — string, keys, a doorknob — which will make Nerai's whole chest hurt the first time she sees it, and she won't know why. *(We will: it's a human version of collecting sunk things.)*
- **The house rule that shocks Nerai:** people hug here. Mid-sentence, for no occasion, on your way past. The first time it happens to her it should read like a small collision she doesn't know the customs for.

## What they are to the story (the refuge — canon)
Their house is **Nerai's respite** — the one place with no factions, no whispers, no being anyone's proof of anything. And here's their superpower, the thing that makes them radical: **they never once ask her what she is.** The whole world is asking. The news is asking. Both oceans are asking. Iggy's mom asks if she wants seconds. That's it. That's the whole miracle.

They figure things out, obviously — they're quirky, not stupid. When the truth lands, their reaction should be the best beat in that chapter: utterly unimpressed, entirely unchanged. Another place at the table. *("From the sea, huh. Long way. You'll want more potatoes.")*

## How they work on the page
- Small doses, huge weight. A dinner scene per act, roughly — each one landing exactly when Nerai's other worlds are squeezing hardest.
- Comedy through obliviousness that isn't: they seem to miss the significance of everything and quietly miss nothing.
- They love Iggy loudly and embarrassingly, which is why Iggy is who he is — the boy who leaves his door open learned it from a whole house that never closes.
- Contrast engine: every scene in their kitchen makes the deep's cold parenting say itself, without the book saying a word. (Lesson 3: feeling in things — here, the thing is a full plate.)

## Only child — so he thinks (canon + a parked maybe, Tim, Jul 10, 2026)
**Canon:** Iggy is an only child. As far as he knows. As far as anyone's said.

**The parked maybe (later books — or never; we reserve the right to not go this route):** there is more behind the jovial demeanor. Two people this relentlessly warm, this unshakeable in the face of impossible things, this practiced at setting an extra place — maybe that's just who they are. Or maybe the warmth is *load-bearing.* Flavors, if we ever pull the thread:
- **A child before Iggy** — the extra place at the table was someone's, once. The hug-ambushes are a policy written in grief.
- **They've seen the impossible before** — their unimpressed reaction to a girl from the sea isn't temperament, it's *experience.* (It would also quietly answer where an ordinary family got a fire-carrying son.)
- **Or nothing at all** — they're exactly what they seem, and in a book where every institution hides something, two people with no secret is its own kind of twist.

**Rule while undecided:** write them so all three readings stay true. Warmth first, always. If we never fire this thread, the book loses nothing.
