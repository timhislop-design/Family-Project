# Iggy — The Firelighter
*(First full file, Jul 10, 2026 — assembled from everything he's already done on the page. He earned it. Clay.)*

## Who he is
Human. A firelighter — one of the gifted, though his gift is honestly a modest one and he has never once cared. He carries a little flame around at night the way other kids carry a phone, no bigger than a marble, just for the light of it. He knocks twice and opens the door anyway, because he has never once managed to wait. He is the kind of boy who does the loyal and foolish thing.

## What he looks like *(Tim, Jul 10, 2026)*
**Lanky, thin, awkward** — all elbows, ears, and neck, a boy assembled slightly ahead of schedule. Clothes fit him nowhere. He trips on flat ground. **Series note: he grows into it** — the frame that's an ambush waiting to happen; somewhere around Book 2 or 3 people start doing double-takes and Iggy has no idea why. (Nerai, of course, will be the last to notice. See below.)

## What he is to Nerai
**Her best friend. Full stop — from her side.** (Tim, Jul 10, 2026:) The tension is entirely on HIS side, and she never sees it — not in this book, not for several. His looks-a-beat-too-long, the door left open, the waiting at the wire: the reader clocks it early; Nerai reads all of it as just Iggy being Iggy. When she finally does see it (book whatever), the whole history rereads itself. Until then: never let her notice, never let him say it, and let his tell stay physical and small.

The only person on dry land who ever left his door open just to hear if she was alright. He saw one true inch of her and has no idea it's the edge of a mile — and when the sea stood up and she said *"they're mine,"* he didn't run, he went very still and stayed exactly where he was, which is the bravest thing anyone does in Chapter 1.

He keeps her secret the way he does everything: loudly. Badly. Completely.

## His home — her refuge (Tim, Jul 10, 2026)
Iggy's parent(s) are the opposite of the deep: warm, loud, unimpressed, dinner-on-the-table people. Their house becomes **Nerai's respite** — the one place with no factions, no whispers, no being anyone's proof of anything. Somebody's mom or dad who just refills her plate and asks how school was. She doesn't have a word for what that feels like at first. That's the point. (Open for Abby: one parent or two? What do they do? What's the house rule that shocks Nerai — like, people HUG here?)

## How he works on the page
- **Humor is his armor and his tell.** The grin arrives "right on schedule" — and when it drops, the reader knows things are serious before Nerai says so.
- **He looks a beat too long, then pretends he didn't.** The love thread (innocent stage) lives in that beat and nowhere else for now.
- **He waits at the wire.** "If you're not back by dark, I'm coming in loud." That's the whole boy in one sentence.
- **His flame is the mood ring of the book.** It gutters when the deep is close; it snuffs when he's scared; someday, when it matters most, it will stand up straight.

## Where he's headed (series, loose)
Book 1: her first friend becomes her first *defender* in front of both worlds — and hesitates exactly once (Ch. 17), which costs him and teaches him.
