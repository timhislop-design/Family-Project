# THE DEEP
### The Premise

---

Every myth humans tell is true. There are children who can call fire into their open hands, pull water out of the air, lean on the wind, or fold the dark around them like a coat — and quiet schools, hidden behind ordinary walls, that take them in and teach them to pass for human. The world has decided that *this* is the whole of the strangeness. It lets everyone sleep.

It isn't.

The oldest secret was never in a castle or a forest. It's under the ocean. The first people never left the water — they've lived in the deep since before there was anything to come back to, watching us learn to walk and lie and build our small stories about how strange the world is allowed to be. They are not monsters and not gods. They are older, and patient, and they have kept one law above all others: **do not interrupt.** Let the surface become whatever it's going to become.

But the waiting has gone on so long it has become a question, and the question has split them in two. The **Abyssals** say *never* — the surface will never be ready, and to rise is to be feared, hunted, ended. The **Pelagics** say *now* — that a secret kept too long stops being wisdom and starts being a wound, and that it is, at long last, about time.

A few of their children are already hidden ashore, enrolled among the *other* exceptional kids. One of them is **Nerai** — fifteen, born in the dark and sent up into the light by her own parents. They were progressive enough to doubt their people's fearful, hidden ways, and frightened enough, when their strange daughter turned out to be the living proof those ways were ending, to send her away and call it protection. For three hundred and four days she has not known whether she was sent up to come home, or sent up to be gone. Her gift is her voice: she questions what everyone else accepts. What else she is, no one ever told her — they made sure of that.

She is about to find out, on the night the sea steps back.

---

*The Deep — Book 1 of a planned series. A coming-out story disguised as a monster story; a girl who refuses the inherited version of the world.*
