# Book Brief — THE DEEP (working title)

*Fill this in together. It's okay to leave parts blank and come back later.*

## The big idea (in Dad's words)
> Take the "school for exceptional kids" premise (Star Wars / Harry Potter / Twilight all share it) — powers, vampires, werewolves, the school that hides them. All of that is true. But add sci-fi: the first people have always been here, living in the deep oceans, wanting to come out. Factions argue over whether the world is ready. Everything else is true too — humans just aren't ready to understand this last reality. But it's about time.

## What kind of book is it?
- Picture book / chapter book / comic / poem / something else: **Chapter book / YA**
- Funny, scary, cozy, exciting, magical...? **Wonder + tension; a coming-out story disguised as a monster story**

## Direction (updated Jul 10, 2026 — supersedes Jun 19)
**De-Avatar the gifts.** No elemental taxonomy — no "water-callers / wind-listeners / fire-handed" classes; that reads as borrowed ATLA. Instead: every gifted kid has **one strange thing, no two alike, no categories** — a boy who carries a marble of fire (Iggy stays; it's HIS, not a class), a girl who warms a cup between her palms, a kid who is never lost, someone the dogs obey. Gifts are personal, odd, unclassifiable — which also makes Nerai's own strangeness easier to hide in. (Still moved away from vampire/werewolf framing.)

## Voice (updated Jul 10, 2026 — Tim's correction)
The chapters were coming out too polished — professional-novelist sentences, no mess, nothing a first-time writer would actually do. Wrong. **The book should sound like the characters, not like an author.** Nerai's narration = an actual fifteen-year-old thinking: run-ons when she spirals, fragments when she's scared, self-interruptions, blunt teenage comparisons instead of literary ones, petty specific observations. Dialogue and action build the story; narration just connects. The narrator's landscape/overview voice lives in the prologue — after that, oversight only, never performance. Personal beats polished, every time.

## Who's in it?
- Main character: **Nerai** — a girl of the deep, hidden ashore at the school, homesick for the water
- Other characters: **Iggy** (human firelighter classmate, loyal); **her father** (Pelagic, leads the surfacing); **her mother** (Abyssal, told her to stay hidden); **Coach Reyes** (school staff)

## Where does it happen?
A coastal school for exceptional kids, right on the ocean line.

## What happens? (just a sentence or two for now)
The sea pulls back and Nerai's people rise to surface for the first time. She has to choose between the secret she was raised to keep and the home she's been homesick for.

## The failing deep (Abby's idea — adopted Jul 10, 2026)
Whatever has always kept the deep livable is **failing — suddenly, without warning, cause unknown.** Some of the deep don't believe it, or call it exaggerated. This changes the argument: the Pelagics aren't just impatient idealists anymore, and the surfacing may not be a choice. It's Book 1's mystery engine. (What exactly is failing — the living light, the song, the water itself — TBD with the magic system. Possible thread: the failing began around Nerai's birth → see the rumor in `characters/nerai.md`.)

## The factions
- **Abyssals** — the deep ones; say humanity will never be ready. (Nerai's mother's side.)
- **Pelagics** — upper-water; say it's time to surface. (Nerai's father's side.)
- Governed by **elders** — no royalty. Why the factions have existed this long is a held-back truth, revealed (and darkening) book over book.

## Secrecy: the rumor tier (decided Jul 10, 2026)
Nobody on the surface *knows* — but everybody's heard something. Sightings off fishing boats, shapes on sonar laughed off the news, encounter stories from people who are never taken seriously again, threads and theories. It reads like today's rumor cycle about the deep sea: believers dismissed, evidence mounting. **Why it's mounting: the Pelagics have grown careless** — some impatience, maybe some of it on purpose — and every leak is a scandal below, sharpening the strife between the factions. So when the sea steps back in Chapter 1, half the world gasps and the other half says *I told you so.*
Mirror below (Abby's prophecy idea, made rumor): the deep runs on the same fuel — whispers about the girl born the year the deep first shivered. No written prophecy anywhere. Both worlds believe and refuse stories the same way, which *is* the theme.

## The map (decided Jul 10, 2026)
The world is a **water column** — depth is politics. But Book 1 keeps its feet in **two main places**:
1. **The trench city** (Abyssal) — Nerai's home, the grown glowing deep.
2. **The coastal school town** — the surface, the tide line.
Mentioned but not visited: **the drowned ruins** in the shallows — the old meeting terraces, and *way older than anyone admits*, older than the deep's own history says anything should be (quiet evidence the Shattering wasn't the first — see `mythology.md`). Held for **Book 2**: the **Pelagic city of the reaches**, the upper-water world between trench and surface. Cities unnamed so far — naming session with Abby.

## Tone (decided Jul 10, 2026)
**Book 1 is light** — wonder, rumor, first contact, found friends. Each book darkens as the real reason for the factions (and the failing) surfaces. The ladder lives in `arc.md`.
