# How We Work Together

*A living page. Dad and Claude keep adding to it as we learn what works. It's also here for Abby — so she can see what a real collaboration looks like and know she has one too.*

## The heart of it
A collaborator isn't a vending machine. You don't ask once and take what you get. You go **back and forth**: ask, react, push back, ask again. The good stuff comes out on the third or fourth pass, not the first. Dad loves to challenge Claude — that's not being difficult, that's how the work gets better.

## How Dad and Claude draft
1. **Start from real prose.** Give Claude an actual passage — a paragraph Abby wrote, a page from a book she loves, a voice memo typed up. Real words beat a blank page every time.
2. **Ask for a chapter (or scene, or page).** "Write the next chapter in this voice." "Try it darker." "Make this character funnier."
3. **React honestly.** "Too long." "That's not how she'd talk." "Closer, but the ending's flat." Specific beats polite.
4. **Go again.** Claude re-drafts on the feedback. Repeat until it's true.
5. **Editor's call.** Dad shapes the final. Abby decides if it's right.

## What Abby can learn from watching
- You're allowed to challenge your collaborator. Say what's wrong. Ask for another try.
- The first draft is never the point — it's clay. You shape it together.
- Your taste *is* the skill. Knowing "that's not right" is half of writing.
- A collaborator remembers, keeps up, and never gets tired of trying again. Use that.
