✦ The pitch, fast

Imagine a world where religion works. Not "people believe it works" — it works. Sing the harvest blessing and it rains. Every time. For two thousand years.

The sky is a dome — an actual roof, the way people in the ancient world truly thought the sky was — with waters above it and stars that are lights. And some of the stars move. Everyone knows those are angels, because you can watch them cross the dark. Nobody in this world has ever doubted. Why would they? Their prayers get answered.

Now the girl. Selah, fifteen. She's a canter — one of the kids trained to memorize the oldest versions of the prayers, the unabridged ones, word-perfect. She's the best of her generation. And she starts noticing something nobody else has noticed in centuries: the old words and the everyday words don't get the same answers.

Her secret — the one she's never told anyone: seven years ago, the night before the great ceremony where the Voice chooses the best young people and takes them up to heaven — the highest honor there is, and her own brother was chosen — little Selah, showing off for God, sang the oldest blessing she knew over him while he slept. He was chosen at dawn. Everyone said the family was blessed. She has spent seven years wondering if she's the one who did it.

Oh — and one person came back from heaven, once. He came back wrong. He lives at the edge of town, nobody is allowed to talk to him... and sometimes, in his sleep, he recites old prayers even Selah doesn't know.

✦ Why we went this direction

You know how The Deep is a "the world is BIGGER than they told you" story? This is the opposite kind — a "the world is SMALLER than they told you" story, like Hunger Games or Silo. We made two rules to keep it ours:

The world is beautiful.
Not gloomy, not gray. Selah loves her world and the reader should too — which makes the mystery scarier, not the world meaner. Festivals, harvests, answered prayers, proud weeping mothers at the choosing.
Selah has no powers.
No prophecy. No chosen one. Her superpower is that she pays attention and remembers. Everything she figures out, she earns.
